Dirac Notation:

The standard notation for a state vector is a ket vector |. For example, a vector in with basis ,, is typically written as

= a + b + c = (a, b, c)T.

In Dirac notation, it would be written as

| v = a| i + b| j + c| k = (a, b, c)T.

The term ket and this notation come from the physicist Paul Dirac who wanted a concise way of writing formulas involving row and column vectors. He referred to row vectors as bra vectors represented as y|. The inner product of a bra and a ket vector is written y| x, and is called a bracket [18].
