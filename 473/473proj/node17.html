Theorem 1

Theorem 1: Given the state vector of our register with one state with amplitude k, and every other state with amplitude l, after an application of A:

  • the amplitude in the one state is k' = - 1k + 2l
  • the amplitude of the remaining (N - 1) states is l' = k + l

Proof: Given the definition of A as Aij = 2/N if i j and Aii = - 1 + 2/N, it follows from the definition of matrix multiplication of k and l by A that:

  • k' = - 1k + 2l
  • l' = l + k + l
The second expression simplifies to l' = k + l with some simple algebraic manipulation. [Grover96]
